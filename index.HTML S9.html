Difference Between Jainism and Buddhism

Jainism and Buddhism are two important ancient Indian religions that emerged as a reform movement in the 6th century BC. Both religions believed in non-violence, karma, and the cycle of rebirth. However, they differ in their approaches to liberation, with Buddhism emphasizing the Middle Path and Jainism advocating asceticism and non-possession.

Features Jainism Buddhism
Founder Lord Mahavira (24th Tirthankara) Siddhartha Gautama, the Buddha
Central Concept Liberation (moksha) through self-discipline Nirvana (liberation from suffering) through the Eightfold Path
Language of Scriptures Ardhamagadhi Pali (Tripitaka)
Religious Text Agamas Tripitaka
Fourfold Path Right faith, right knowledge, right conduct, asceticism The Noble Eightfold Path
Existence of Soul Belief in eternal Jiva (soul) Denial of self (anatta)
Approach to Truth Anekantavada: The truth is multifaceted Middle Way: Avoid extremes, seek balance
Role of Monks/Nuns Strict codes of conduct, celibacy Disciplined monastic life
Worship Practices Idol worship of Tirthankaras No idol worship, emphasis on meditation
Division into Sects Svetambara and Digambara Mahayana and Theravada
Spread of Religion Limited mainly to India Spread across Asia and beyond
