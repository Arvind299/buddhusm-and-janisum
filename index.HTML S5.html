Timeline Comparison: Buddhism & Jainism (6th Century BCE)

Buddhism

Founder: Siddhartha Gautama (Buddha)

Born: c. 563 BCE in Lumbini (modern-day Nepal)

Major Events:

  • 534 BCE: Siddhartha renounces his royal life in search of enlightenment.
  • 528 BCE: Attains enlightenment under the Bodhi tree in Bodh Gaya.
  • 483 BCE: Buddha passes away (Parinirvana) in Kushinagar.

Teachings: The Four Noble Truths, The Eightfold Path, and the Middle Way.

Jainism

Key Figure: Lord Mahavira (24th Tirthankara)

Born: c. 599 BCE in Kundagrama (modern-day Bihar, India)

Major Events:

  • 569 BCE: Renounces worldly life and becomes an ascetic.
  • 557 BCE: Attains Kevala Jnana (omniscience) after 12 years of intense meditation.
  • 527 BCE: Mahavira attains Moksha (liberation) at the age of 72.

Teachings: Ahimsa (non-violence), Satya (truth), Aparigraha (non-attachment), and the concept of Karma.
